Pumpkin Pie

Introduction

The pumpkin was an early export to France; from there it was introduced to Tudor England, and the flesh of the "pompion" was quickly accepted as pie filling. During the seventeenth century, pumpkin pie recipes could be found in English cookbooks, such as Hannah Woolley's The Gentlewoman's Companion (1675).

A Few Facts About Punpkin Pie

Pumpkins were first cultivated in Central America around 5,500 BC. The British introduced pumpkin pie to society in the mid-1500s, and the Pilgrims brought it to America in the early 1600s. Pumpkin pie became a Thanksgiving staple in the early 18th century.

The first pumpkin pies were whole baked pumpkins stuffed with apples and spices.

In 2010, the New Bremen Giant Pumpkin Growers in Ohio made the world's largest pumpkin pie, which weighed 3,699 pounds and was 20 feet in diameter.

Pumpkin was used to make bread without much flour.

The Christmas songs "There's No Place Like Home for the Holidays","Rockin' Around The Christmas Tree", and "Sleigh Ride" all mention pmpkin pie.

According to a survey by the American Pie Council, apple is the favorite pie, with 19% of Americans choosing it, while pumpkin is in second place with 13%.

Pumpkin Pie Recipe

Ingredients

1 Pastry Crust

2 large eggs

2 c.pumpkin puree

11/2 c.granulated sugar

1 tsp cinnamon

14oz can sweetened condensed milk

1/4 tsp nutmeg

1/4 tsp ground cloves

1/2 tsp ground ginger

1/2 tsp salt

Baking Instructions

1. Prepare pie plate with pastry crust - homemade or store-bought.

2. Preheat oven to 375 .

3. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs and all remaining until fully combined.

4. Pour the pumpkin pie filling into prepared pie crust.

5. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the edges of the filling have set and the center is just barely loose.

6. (a knife inserted halfway between the edge of the pie and the center should come out clean) and the pastry should be golden brown

7. Remove from oven and allow to cool on wire rack before slicing

8. Serve with whipped cream. (optional)

* 8-10 serves

TIP: You may need to cover the edges of your pie with tin foil to prevent browning. Grab a piece of tin foil bag enough to cover pie. Fold in half. Cut semi-circle on folded edge of foil. (like you used to make hearts in elementary school). Open and you should have a piece of tin foil with a big hole in it. Cover pie if needed.
